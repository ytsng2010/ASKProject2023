    Malaysia art and culture

    Malaysia is a multi-racial country that lives in peace. The three main races in Malaysia are the Malays, Chinese and Indian.

    Malays are the largest race, making up 54% of Malaysia's population. The Malays are a Muslim race, speak Malay and practice Malay culture. Chinese represent 25% of the Malaysian population. Most Chinese are Buddhist, Taoist or Christian, speak Mandarin, Hokkien, Hakka, Cantonese or Teochew. Indians make up 7.5% of the population. Most are Hindu, speak Tamil, Malayalam, or Hindi.

              The largest race in Sarawak is the Iban which has a total population of 1,300,000 in Malaysia. There are 170,000 Bidayuh people live in the southwest of Sarawak. The largest tribe in Sabah is the Kadazandusun, which has approximately 1,700,000 people in Malaysia. A total of 140,000 Orang Asli also live in Malaysia. In addition, there are also Sikhs, Serani or Eurasians and other bumiputera races.

            Each race and religion represents a unique culture. In that culture it also represents a special art that has been passed down through the ages. Let's get to know the art culture Malaysia together in this page.
